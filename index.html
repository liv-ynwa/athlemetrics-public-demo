Models & tools

Athlemetrics is organised around three core research tracks: player rating, role & similarity profiling, and tactical insight. Each track is grounded in per-90 statistics and position-specific feature sets.

Model track

Player Rating Demo

Multi-layer rating models for attackers, midfielders, defenders, and goalkeepers using per-90 metrics, logistic scaling, and a critic layer to adjust scores for role context.

Read technical notes in the blog →
Model track

Player Similarity & Profiling

Role-aware embeddings and clustering (e.g. forwards, midfielders, defenders, goalkeepers) to search for players with similar playing styles, usage patterns, and tactical roles.

Tactical & Performance Insight

Who Athlemetrics is for

The platform is built for anyone who needs a clear, data-driven view of player contribution — from curious fans to analysts and engineering teams building their own tools.

Analysts & Data Teams

Use model outputs and similarity search to support scouting, recruitment shortlists, or internal reports in a consistent, reproducible way.

Scouts & Sporting Directors

Quickly understand how a player’s style and profile compare to existing squad members or archetypal roles before deeper video work.

Coaches & Performance Staff

Translate complex metrics into intuitive concepts like “chance creation”, “ball progression”, or “defensive disruption” to inform tactical decisions.

Fans & Creators

Explore data-backed views of your favourite players and teams, and use visualisations in your own analysis threads, blogs, or videos.

Engineers & Builders

Integrate Athlemetrics APIs into your own dashboards, bots, or tools, or contribute new ideas and experiments through the codebase.

Researchers

Treat Athlemetrics as a living lab for football data science — test new models, compare approaches, or build on top of existing pipelines.
